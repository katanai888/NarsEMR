# BansaEMR

### Filipino Dashboard EMR for Small Clinics and Stand-alone Medical Centers
**BansaEMR para sa mga maliliit na klinika at hiwalay na mga medical center.**

---

## 📋 Project Overview

**BansaEMR** is a specialized, standalone Electronic Medical Records (EMR) and practice management solution engineered by B-Technologies🇵🇭, acting as the ASEAN development arm of Medverse Labs. It is optimized specifically for the unique clinical and administrative workflows of independent healthcare facilities, rural health units (RHUs), and small clinics across the Philippines. 

The system introduces a highly tailored user experience designed to make medical charting, scheduling, and clinic management intuitive, efficient, and accessible for local healthcare workers.

---

## ✨ Key Features

* **Localization:** Integrated EN/TL (English/Tagalog) global language framework across all core clinic and dashboard views to bridge the technical literacy gap for community nurses and administrative staff.
* **Streamlined Dashboard:** A simplified interface engineered specifically for the fast-paced nature of standalone Filipino medical centers.
* **Comprehensive Care Management:** Full standalone access to electronic charting, scheduling, demographics, billing modules, and practice management tools without external system dependencies.

---

## 🩺 Significance and Impact in Philippine Healthcare

The Philippine healthcare landscape remains highly fragmented, with over 60% of primary care encounters occurring in small, independent clinics. Despite rapid digital transformation in major apex hospitals, small community clinics remain severely underserved.

### 1. Bridging the Digital Divide
Out of the estimated 40,000+ private clinics and Rural Health Units (RHUs) across the archipelago, fewer than 20% utilize a functional, localized EMR system. The vast majority still rely on manual, paper-based charting. BansaEMR provides an accessible, standalone path to digitization.

### 2. Reducing Administrative Burden
Independent practitioners spend up to 40% of their working hours on administrative upkeep and tracking loose paper patient records. BansaEMR optimizes these workflows, heavily reducing paperwork and increasing direct patient face-time.

### 3. Population Health & Universal Health Care (UHC) Alignment
* **Continuity of Care:** Digitizing records reduces diagnostic duplication and medication errors in a country where out-of-pocket health expenditure sits at over 40%.
* **Syndromic Surveillance:** Transitioning clinics from paper to structured digital data lays the groundwork for tracking localized outbreaks (such as Dengue, Leptospirosis, or respiratory infections) to improve municipal health responses.
* **UHC Act Integration:** BansaEMR structures clinical data workflows to align with the Philippines' Universal Health Care Act (RA 11223), helping small providers transition seamlessly into data-driven primary care networks.
